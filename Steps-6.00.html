The report indicates that between 2012 to 2022 the trend of the percentage of total deaths of Police in the USA has been fluctuating.

Reducing police deaths in the line of duty is a complex issue that requires a multifaceted approach. Here are some suggestions that could help decrease the number of police deaths:

1. Increasing training and Implementing stricter gun control measures :

 According to a report, 544(21%) of police officers who died in the line of duty were killed by firearms. Missouri and Kentucky both have a 300% increase in percentage compared to the same period last year. Therefore, the police should be adequately trained in de-escalation techniques, community policing, and conflict resolution. This can help reduce the likelihood of confrontations escalating into violent situations. And also, stricter gun control laws can help prevent dangerous individuals from obtaining firearms, reducing the number of firearms in circulation and the likelihood of shootings.

2. Regular training for safe driving:

In the report,479(18)% of Police officer deaths occurred during traffic-related incidents. Automobile crash takes the highest portion 272(56%) of traffic-related deaths (showed a decrease of 17% in 2020). followed by Struck by Vehicle 82(17%).
  • Therefore, Provide comprehensive and regular training to police officers on safe driving techniques and defensive driving strategies.
  • Implement strict policies and procedures for high-speed pursuits to minimize the risks associated with these types of incidents.
  • Equip police vehicles with the latest safety technology, such as automatic emergency braking systems, lane departure warning systems, and backup cameras.
3. Better community relations:

 In this report assault including vehicular assault caused 5% of the total death in the past decade. A report by the National Institute of Justice found that police departments with better community relations had fewer officer deaths. By building trust with the communities they serve, police officers may be less likely to encounter violent situations that could lead to their deaths.

4. Promote healthy lifestyles & Provide health screenings:
 
Heart attacks caused 7% of the total death with an avg 6% increase of every year. To address this problem  Police departments can encourage officers to adopt healthier lifestyles, including regular exercise, a healthy diet, and stress reduction techniques such as mindfulness and meditation. And, Regular health screenings, including blood pressure, cholesterol, and diabetes checks, can help identify officers at risk of heart disease and other illnesses. Early detection and treatment can help prevent more serious health issues.

5. Showing respect: 

The simplest way to show respect for police officers is by obeying the law. When you follow traffic laws, for example, you help ensure the safety of yourself and others on the road, which in turn can reduce the need for police intervention.
  • Be polite: If you are stopped by a police officer, be polite and courteous. This can help ease the situation and make it easier for the officer to do their job.
