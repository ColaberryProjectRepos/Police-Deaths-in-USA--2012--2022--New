  •   On the Power BI desktop under Modeling click on the new table button.
  •    type the Dax formula below in the Dax formula bar, click on the check mark, and save.
Calendar = ADDCOLUMNS ( CALENDARAUTO (),
 
"year",year([Date]),
 
    "MonthNo", MONTH([Date]),
 
    "Month", FORMAT([Date],"mmm"),
 
    "Quarter", FORMAT([Date],"\QQ"),
 
    "YearMonth", FORMAT([Date],"YYYY-MM"),  "YearMonthNAME", FORMAT([Date],"YYYY/mmm"),
 
    "WeekdayNo", WEEKDAY([Date],2),
 

    "Weekday", FORMAT([Date],"ddd"))

Step 5.   Creating Refresh Date Table
 
1. On the Power BI Desktop ribbon, Click on Transform data
2.  Click on the New Source button, and select Blank query from the list and click on Connect
3.  Under properties in the Name box type in 'Refresh Date'
4.  On the Dax formula bar type ' Refresh Date=DateTime.LocalNow()'
5.  Click on the Convert to Table button 
6.  After the refresh table pops up, rename the column by right-clicking and  
      choosing to rename from the selection name ’DateTime’
7. Click on the Close & Apply button and save.
